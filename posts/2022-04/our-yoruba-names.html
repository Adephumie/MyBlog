Fola and Dad

As a Nigerian from the Yoruba ethnic group living in the abroad, I thought of putting out our yoruba names and their meanings. This will help me, my husband, and the kids to remember these meanings when asked. We meet people of diverse cultures, nationalities, and races and the first thing they want to know is our name followed by the meaning. When I was in Nigeria, I never had the need to actively know the meaning of my name. So that question always caught me off-guard. But with this blogpost, I'd have been able to achieve this aim.

Yorubas have a culture where the names given have powerful meanings. Many things depict the Yoruba culture and our names are usually a combination of two or more of these words: Oluwa (God), Ade (Crown), Ola (Wealth), Ayo (Joy), Olu (God), Iyi (Honor), Ire (Goodness), Omo (Child) etc. We are a family of four and I have picked our names to elaborate on their meanings.
