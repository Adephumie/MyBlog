My Journey into Tech

My name is Funmilayo A. Fagoyinbo, my tech journey started passively in 2020, and towards the end of 2021, I became active in my pursuit of becoming a DevOps/Cloud Engineer. I bagged my Bachelor of Science degree in Chemistry from Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile-Ife, Osun state, in 2012. And months after I completed my NYSC (a program set up by the Nigerian government for graduates), I started my career as a Medical Sales Representative in the Pharmaceutical Industry in Nigeria. I gained some valuable and transferable skills being a Med Rep and some of them include:

  • Excellent communication skills with customers- knowing that they are the lifeline of the business.
  • Ability to use my initiative when dealing with customers, colleagues, and managers.
  • Great attention to detail and ensuring my bookkeeping and accounts are up to date.
  • Commitment to planning: This entails planning my itinerary for the week and month thereby helping me manage my activities in my assigned region.
  • Team Effort: I also learnt to follow up with work and collaborate with my team which included colleagues and managers to ensure the success of our short and long term goals.

Aside from my profession as a Medical Sales Rep., I am also a Creative- a Fashion Designer. While I was an undergraduate and did not have any sewing skills, I would create a mental picture of my look, sketch it, and give it to a tailor to make for me. So, after I completed my NYSC program and could not get any job placement, I decided to start my fashion designing education and I enrolled in a fashion school. I enjoyed the creativity in the process of garment making that entailed designing, sketching, and sewing. The part of the course I was most excited about was the pattern drafting aspect that entailed measurements, mathematical conversions, and construction (taking me back to my technical drawing and building construction background). I have always loved delving into technical things and that was what made the pattern drafting aspect of the fashion design more appealing to me.

I would sketch out designs, proceed to make patterns for the clothes, cut them out, and use them to construct beautiful garments for clients. Due to my love for business and a desire to provide solutions, I started making beddings such as pillowcases, bedspreads, and duvets based on orders. I also made and sold beddings for babies and older children. I was also able to add more skills to myself (handling my business and its intricacies). I had to learn to meet up with deadlines, multitask, and as my career as a Med. Rep. began, I learnt to prioritize.

However, according to two of my best quotes, “Life is a journey, not a destination” and “Learning is a constant process of discovery- a process without end”, I knew I wanted more for myself. I wanted an opportunity that would challenge me, show me that I could set goals and meet them, and ultimately, something I could grow into. I loved challenges, was not afraid if it had to do with mathematics or calculation, loved the idea of contributing to the advancement of projects, and then, an opportunity came- I relocated with my family.

The relocation made it possible for me to start on a fresh page, and as a wife and mum of two, I knew I wanted to switch to a career that would create a good work-life balance. Also, after the Covid-19 pandemic, remote and hybrid work became a thing. So, it was a perfect switch that would also bring together all the experiences and skills I had gathered over the years. This gave me the clarity I needed on what new challenge I could take on.

Another major reason why I wanted to take on this new challenge and see myself succeed is because of my children, especially my daughter. Years from now, I want her to see a figure she can relate to. I want to be able to tell her that she can aim for the stars and be anything she aspires to be. I do not want to be in a position where I would tell her she could be anything she wanted to be and then she would ask if I was able to achieve my dreams, and I would say, "No". I want her to be proud to call me her role model.

So, No, I do not have everything figured out yet, but I know it is the beginning of many greater things to come. I am looking forward to celebrating 5, 10, 15, and more years of working as an Engineer. I recently took my AWS Cloud Practitioner certification exam and I passed ( I was so excited, I still am). I am learning more about Front End Development, Python, Linux, etc. This journey has taught me a lot; some of whicheer are to be grateful and celebrate the little wins that will eventually become the bigger things. I am so excited about what the future holds and I am looking forward to its unfolding. Thank you.
